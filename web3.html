Cryptocurrency vs the Australian Dollar


What Is Cryptocurrency?

• Cryptocurrency is a form of digital money that exists only online and is managed using blockchain technology

• It can be stored in digital wallets and transferred directly between users without the need for banks

• Transactions are recorded on the blockchain, which makes them secure and transparent, but also irreversible

• Unlike traditional money, cryptocurrency is not controlled by any central authority or government.


How the Australian Dollar Works

• The Australian Dollar (AUD), on the other hand, is managed and regulated by the Reserve Bank of Australia

• Everyday Australians can trust the Australian Dollar because it is widely accepted, backed by a central authority, and subject to legal protections

• Transactions are also reversible in some cases, such as with banking disputes, which adds an extra layer of safety.


Whats are the Risks of Switching to Cryptocurrency

• Replacing the Australian Dollar with cryptocurrency would introduce significant risks

• Cryptocurrency values can fluctuate wildly within hours, creating instability for everyday expenses like groceries or rent

• Users could also face digital risks, including hacking, losing access to wallets, or accidental errors that cannot be reversed

• Without government control, there would be no safety net for users in cases of fraud, theft, or major system failures.

Impact on Everyday Australians

• Everyday users would be heavily affected by such a switch

• Paying bills, earning wages, and saving money would become more complicated and less predictable

• While cryptocurrencies offer benefits like decentralisation and privacy, these advantages do not outweigh the stability, trust, and protections provided by the Australian Dollar

Conclusion

For these reasons, Australia should continue using its national currency rather than replacing it with cryptocurrency.
